# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Businesses and individuals active in — or looking to join — Paraguay's digital commerce sector. They range from early-stage ecommerce entrepreneurs to established companies and service providers (marketing agencies, developers, UX practitioners, SEO specialists). Secondary audience: public and private entities engaging with CAPACE on policy matters such as the Electronic Commerce Law and Digital Invoice. Users arrive to understand what CAPACE offers, to become members, to find events and training, or to make contact.

## Product Purpose

CAPACE (Cámara Paraguaya de Comercio Electrónico) is Paraguay's chamber for electronic commerce. It formalizes and develops the country's digital ecosystem by training professionals, connecting members, running industry events, and advocating with private, public, and international institutions on legislation that shapes ecommerce in Paraguay. Success looks like: a prospective member immediately understanding the value of joining; an existing member finding resources quickly; and a policymaker or partner recognizing CAPACE as the credible, authoritative voice of the sector.

## Brand Personality

Warm, authoritative, and grounded. CAPACE is not a startup — it is a chamber: a serious institution with a human face. It is led by experienced professionals in a young sector, and the brand should carry both the energy of that growth and the credibility of real expertise. Voice is direct and clear in Spanish, with pride in Paraguayan digital progress.

## Anti-references

- Corporate sterility: navy + white palettes, stock photos of handshakes, zero-personality grid layouts, formal boilerplate copy that could belong to any institution.
- Generic SaaS/startup aesthetic: warm cream backgrounds, soft gradient cards, rounded everything, "AI-made" minimalism with no cultural specificity.
- International chamber clichés: flags-in-a-row imagery, UN-adjacent visual language, the "global reach" template that erases local identity.

## Design Principles

1. **Local authority first.** The design should feel distinctly Paraguayan — not a pale imitation of US or European chamber sites. Cultural specificity is a strength, not a limitation.
2. **Community is the product.** Every surface is an invitation to belong. Membership, events, and connections should feel central, not buried under institutional copy.
3. **Warmth with conviction.** Approachable does not mean soft. CAPACE takes positions on legislation and standards; the design should carry that confidence without becoming cold.
4. **Credibility through specificity.** Numbers and milestones earn their place when they are precise and current (170+ members, 200% sales growth since 2014). Decorative stats undermine trust; real ones build it.
5. **Clarity is the brand.** A chamber's site must be easy to navigate for a wide range of users. Simplicity is not a fallback — it is the primary expression of respect for the audience.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Good contrast on all text (≥4.5:1 body, ≥3:1 large text). Keyboard-navigable. Screen reader support for navigation and interactive elements. Reduced motion alternative for any animations. Primary language is Spanish (Paraguay); copy should be written clearly for a broad reading level.
