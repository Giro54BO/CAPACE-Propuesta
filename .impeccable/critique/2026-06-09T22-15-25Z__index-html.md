## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: The page would not immediately read "AI made this" to a casual viewer — the real photography rescues it. But a trained eye finds three tells: (1) The stats section is the exact structure the brand register bans ("big number, small label, supporting stats" × 4 on a colored band); (2) every section — Qué hacemos, Nuestros socios, Actividades recientes — receives the identical section-header treatment (uppercase tracked heading + 3px blue rule + secondary link), which is the AI scaffold grammar the brand reference calls out by name; (3) single font without a committed typographic reason. ## Overall Impression

A professionally made institutional page with real assets and good bones. The palette is committed and appropriate, the event photography is distinctive, the typography hierarchy is legible. The page falls short of its brand potential in two places: the stats section looks like every other chamber-of-commerce metrics band on the internet, and the section header component is used identically for all three content sections — producing a rhythm that reads as template output, not editorial design. Fix those two patterns and the page earns its own voice.

## What's Working

**1. Event photography as the design.** Using real photos (CPG05489, _CE_6015, CPG05470) instead of stock imagery is the single biggest differentiator. The hero image with an engaged woman laptop-at-lap and audience behind her is exactly the right choice — it communicates learning community, not broadcast. The 1:1 cards in the news grid let the photos lead.

**2. The palette is committed.** `#181c36` (deep navy) as a structural color with `#e40032` (red) as the action accent is a strong institutional pairing. The socios section using the same navy as the stats band creates a contained dark-register thread through the page. The OKLCH tinting on muted text is careful work.

**3. Pillar section editorial layout.** The three-column tag / heading / body row with `border-top` dividers and column-grid breakpoints is genuinely editorial. The pillar heading changing to red-dark on hover gives a precise interactive reward without excess motion.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Stats section is the hero-metric template

**What**: Four centered big numbers on a dark blue band with uppercase labels. This is the exact structure the brand register bans: "Big number, small label, supporting stats, gradient accent." The gradient accent is absent, but the composition is identical. Any visitor who has seen a dozen landing pages will read this as boilerplate.

**Why it matters**: The stats section is the highest-density brand signal on the page — it's the proof point for CAPACE's impact. Presenting it as a generic metrics band wastes that opportunity and makes the real Hot Sale numbers feel less credible than they are.

**Fix**: Break the centered-grid presentation. Options: (a) An editorial horizontal list where each stat has a short sentence of context alongside the number, breaking the 2×2 grid; (b) an asymmetric layout where one dominant number (7MM USD) anchors the left and the three supporting stats run vertically on the right; (c) a sequential reveal that treats the four figures as a narrative — total → stores → buyers → growth. The data is strong; the frame is weak.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout` targeting the stats section specifically.

---

### [P1] Identical section-header grammar on every section is AI scaffold

**What**: All three content sections (Qué hacemos, Nuestros socios, Actividades recientes) use the same `.section-header` component: uppercase tracked heading + 3px blue rule + small red secondary link. The socios section at least lives on a dark background, but the visual grammar is still identical. Brand.md states: "Repeated tiny uppercase tracked labels above every section heading is AI grammar... an eyebrow on every section is AI grammar."

**Why it matters**: Real editorial design varies entry points between sections. Using one repeated component as structural scaffolding produces a page that scans as generated, not designed. The three sections serve very different emotional roles (capability → community → proof), and nothing in their headers signals that difference.

**Fix**: Give each section a distinct visual entry. Options: (a) The pillars section (already strong) has no entry header — let the pillar tags carry the identity and remove the section-header entirely; (b) the socios section on dark blue could use a large inline number ("170 socios") as the heading rather than a label; (c) the news section header is the one place the component makes sense (it has a secondary action link). Consolidate to one section where the component earns its place; break the others out of the template.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout`

---

### [P2] Placeholder links erode trust at conversion moments

**What**: Four `href="#"` links in production markup: Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn (footer social), and "Ver todas →" (news section). These silently do nothing when clicked. The social links have `aria-label` attributes implying real destinations; clicking them and going nowhere breaks the implied promise.

**Why it matters**: CAPACE's primary CTA is "Asociarse" — membership. A prospect clicking around the page before committing will try the social links. Dead links at that moment signal a site that isn't maintained, which directly undermines the credibility case for joining.

**Fix**: Replace `href="#"` with real URLs or, if not yet available, remove the elements rather than showing dead affordances. The "Ver todas →" link in the news section can point to a future `/noticias` page; until it exists, remove it — the section heading alone is sufficient.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden`

---

### [P2] Footer border-radius breaks the sharp design language

**What**: The footer has `border-radius: 20px 20px 0 0`. Every other element on the page uses `border-radius: 0` — buttons, cards, nav. The rounded footer creates a modal-like lifted frame that conflicts with the editorial sharpness established throughout.

**Why it matters**: Consistency of corner language is a low-level but visceral signal of design intentionality. The rounded footer makes it look like a template component dropped in from a different system.

**Fix**: Remove `border-radius` from `.footer` or, if a soft top is desired for the dark band landing, use `clip-path: polygon(0 20px, 20px 0, ...)` or a CSS `mask` approach. The simplest fix is `border-radius: 0`.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable polish`

---

### [P2] `max-height` animation on mobile menu causes layout reflow

**What**: `.mobile-menu` uses `transition: max-height var(--duration-slow)` to animate between `max-height: 0` and `max-height: 420px`. Animating `max-height` forces the browser to recalculate layout on every frame — the opposite of GPU-composited animation.

**Why it matters**: On mid-range Android phones (a significant share of Paraguay's mobile market), this produces visible jank on every menu open/close.

**Fix**: Replace with `display: grid; grid-template-rows: 0fr / 1fr` transition, which does animate performantly, or use `clip-path: inset(0 0 100% 0)` to `clip-path: inset(0 0 0 0)`. The grid-rows approach is the most browser-safe:
```css
.mobile-menu {
  display: grid;
  grid-template-rows: 0fr;
  transition: grid-template-rows var(--duration-slow) var(--ease-out);
}
.mobile-menu > * { overflow: hidden; }
.mobile-menu.is-open { grid-template-rows: 1fr; }
```

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable optimize`

## Minor Observations

- `hero__label` reads "Cámara Paraguaya de Comercio Electrónico" — exact same text as the page `<title>`. It occupies prime real estate above the hero heading for zero new information. Replace with something that carries emotional or narrative weight ("Fundada en 2018 · Más de 170 socios activos" or simply remove it and let the heading lead).
- `#4c5e7b` appears three times as a hardcoded hex (`.hero__lead`, `.pillar__body`, `.pillars .section-heading`). This should be `var(--color-ink-muted)` or a named token — currently it's a maintenance risk.
- Stats item hover scales to 1.05x (`transform: scale(1.05)`). Stats are data display, not interactive affordances. The hover scale implies clickability that doesn't exist; remove it.
- Socios marquee logos that are purely white (e.g., EFX white PNG) become invisible on the dark background at their default filter state (`grayscale(1) brightness(2) opacity(0.5)`). The brightness boost helps, but some logos with no dark variant simply disappear.

## Questions to Consider

- The stats section has real, strong numbers — but no CAPACE narrative around them. What does 70% YoY transaction growth *mean* for a merchant who's deciding whether to join? The number needs a sentence of context, not just a label.
- Three sections use the same entry grammar. If you had to give each section a completely different visual register — different font treatment, different color, different entry pattern — what would each one be? That exercise usually reveals which section template is load-bearing and which is scaffolding.
- The footer has email and phone but no web form. How many contacts per month does CAPACE receive? If it's low, a lightweight form (name + email + "¿Empresa o profesional independiente?") on the page itself might meaningfully increase conversion without requiring a backend.
